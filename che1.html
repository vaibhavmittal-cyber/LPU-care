FOOD WASTAGE 
Food waste refers to the discarding or disposal of edible food at various stages of the food supply chain, from production and processing to distribution, 
retail,and consumption. Food waste includes any food that is discarded, spoiled, or left uneaten, and it can occur for various reasons


Various reason of food wastages
  1. overproduction and Excess Purchases:
    • Market Demand Mismatch
    • Economic Loss
    • Environmental Impact
    • Waste Generation

  2. Lack of Proper Storage:
    • Humidity Levels
    • Temperature Control
    • Neglect of Leftovers
    • Cross-Contamination
  1. Cultural and Social Factors:
    • Generosity and Sharing
    • Food as a Status Symbol
    • Cultural Norms and Expectations
    • Food Abundance and Prosperity

  2. Lack of Infrastructure and Technology:
    • Lack of Refrigeration
    • Post-Harvest Handling
    • Food Processing
    • Access to Information



Statistics of food wastage

Golobal waste :

 
        Approximately one-third of all food produced for human consumption is lost or wasted globally, which is equivalent to about 
      1.3 billion tons of food each year.

Economic Impact :

         The economic cost of food waste is substantial, estimated at around $1 trillion annually, considering the value of the 
        food itself and the associated costs such as transportation, storage, and disposal.

Household Waste :

 
        In many developed countries, a significant portion of food waste occurs at the household level. In the United States, 
        for example,households were responsible for about 33 million tons of food waste in 2018.

Environmental Impact :

     Food waste has significant environmental consequences. When food is discarded, it ends up in landfills where
         it decomposes  and produces methane,a potent greenhouse gas. Additionally, the resources used to produce, transport, 
         and store wasted food are also wasted, including water, energy, and land.

Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) :

        
        Reducing food waste is linked to several of the United Nations'Sustainable Development Goals,
          including Goal 2 (Zero Hunger) and  Goal 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production).
